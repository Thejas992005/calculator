How to Calculate EMI

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed monthly payment made to repay a loan over a specified tenure. It includes both the principal repayment and the interest on the outstanding loan balance.

EMI = P × r × (1 + r)n / ((1 + r)n – 1)

Where:

  • P = Principal loan amount
  • r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
  • n = Number of monthly installments (loan tenure in months)

Example: For a loan of ₹10,00,000 at 8.5% per annum for 20 years (240 months), the monthly rate is 0.00708. Plugging into the formula gives an EMI of approximately ₹8,678.

Frequently Asked Questions

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment. It is a fixed amount you pay to your lender each month until the full loan is repaid. Each EMI consists of two parts: a portion that goes toward the principal (the original loan amount) and a portion that covers the interest. In the early months, the interest component is larger, and over time the principal component increases while the interest portion decreases.

The interest rate has a significant impact on your EMI. A higher interest rate means a larger portion of each payment goes toward interest, resulting in a higher EMI. Even a small difference of 0.5% can amount to a substantial difference over the loan tenure. For example, on a ₹50 lakh home loan for 20 years, a 0.5% rate increase can add over ₹1,500 to your monthly EMI and lakhs to your total interest paid.

Making a prepayment (a lump-sum payment in addition to your regular EMI) reduces the outstanding principal balance. This can either reduce the remaining tenure of your loan or lower your monthly EMI, depending on the option you choose with your lender. Prepayment is an excellent way to save on total interest costs. Most banks allow prepayment on floating-rate loans without penalty, though fixed-rate loans may carry a prepayment charge.

A shorter tenure means higher monthly EMIs but significantly lower total interest paid over the life of the loan. A longer tenure makes EMIs more affordable each month but results in a much higher total interest outflow. For instance, a ₹30 lakh loan at 9% for 15 years costs about ₹14.5 lakh in interest, while the same loan for 25 years costs approximately ₹26.2 lakh — nearly double. Choose a tenure that balances affordability with total cost.
