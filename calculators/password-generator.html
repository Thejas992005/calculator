Password Security Best Practices

A strong password is your first line of defence against unauthorized access to your accounts and personal data. Here are key principles for creating and managing secure passwords:

  • Use at least 12–16 characters. Longer passwords are exponentially harder to crack through brute-force attacks.
  • Mix character types. Combine uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols to maximize the number of possible combinations.
  • Avoid personal information. Never use names, birthdays, phone numbers, or dictionary words as passwords.
  • Use unique passwords. Never reuse the same password across multiple accounts. A breach on one site could compromise all your accounts.
  • Use a password manager. Password managers generate and store complex passwords securely, so you only need to remember one master password.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA). Even with a strong password, 2FA adds an extra layer of security by requiring a second verification step.
